Understanding HyperText Components

Hyperlinks:

Hyperlinks are the foundation of hypertext. They are the connections between different pieces of information that allow the reader to navigate between them. Hyperlinks are typically activated by a mouse click, keypress set, or screen touch, and are used to connect different pieces of information in a non-linear way. Hyperlinks are one of the key underlying concepts of the World Wide Web, and are used to create interconnected documents that can be easily navigated and explored.

Nodes:

Nodes are the individual pieces of information that are connected by hyperlinks. They can be text, images, tables, or other presentational content formats with integrated hyperlinks. Nodes are the building blocks of hypertext, and are used to create interconnected documents that can be easily navigated and explored. Nodes are often used to represent different pieces of information, and are connected by hyperlinks to create a non-linear structure that allows for easy navigation and exploration.

Anchors:

Anchors are the points within a hypertext document that are linked to other points within the same document or to external documents. They are typically activated by a mouse click, keypress set, or screen touch, and are used to create interconnected documents that can be easily navigated and explored. Anchors are often used to create non-linear structures within a hypertext document, and are connected by hyperlinks to create a non-linear structure that allows for easy navigation and exploration.
